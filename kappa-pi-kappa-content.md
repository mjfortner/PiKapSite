## About

### Founding Principles
The society was established as a literary and debate organization dedicated to core ideals:
- Democracy
- Equality of opportunity
- Loyalty to Dartmouth
- Service

### Evolution
The organization transitioned from a literary and debate society to a social fraternity in 1905, while maintaining its foundational commitments.

### Current Mission
- Brotherhood diversity across different backgrounds
- Charitable and social contributions to Dartmouth College
- Long-term alumni engagement and dedication

### Chairman's Perspective
Greg Smith '85 (MD) reflects that the fraternity's founding principles manifest as genuine friendship, intellectual exchange across diverse viewpoints, and mutual respect among members. He credits the organization with developing both his social and intellectual capacities during his undergraduate years.

## History

### Founders
- Harrison Hobart
- Stephen Gordon Nash
- John Dudley Philbrick

These individuals separated from the United Fraternity ("Fraters") organization to establish their own group.

### Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1842 | Founded on July 13 as Kappa Kappa Kappa (Tri-Kap) |
| 1860-1894 | Located at "The Hall" at the present Hopkins Center for the Arts site |
| 1894 | Relocated to Parker House (current Silsby Hall location) |
| 1905 | Transitioned to a social organization |
| 1923 | Moved to current home at 1 Webster Avenue, establishing the main Hall |
| 2016 | Hall underwent expansion and modernization |
| 2022 | Changed name from Kappa Kappa Kappa to Kappa Pi Kappa |

### Name Change (2022)
Changed official name from Kappa Kappa Kappa to Kappa Pi Kappa to distance itself from a hate organization founded in 1867 with opposing ideals.

### Legacy
The organization's members have included war heroes, business and political leaders, and contributors to communities globally across nearly two centuries.

## Rush / Recruitment

### Core Values
Kappa Pi Kappa emphasizes "loyalty, democracy, equality, and service to the community, to Dartmouth college, and to the brothers of the house."

### Identity
As "the oldest local fraternity in America," Kappa Pi Kappa boasts over 180 years of tradition centered on:
- Academic achievement
- Personal development
- Mutual respect

### Key Message
"No matter your background, there is, and always will be, something for you to enjoy at Kappa Pi Kappa."

### Goal
The fraternity aims to "create a welcoming environment for the entire Dartmouth student body" while empowering members to become leaders.

### Donation Methods

#### Dollar-a-Day Programs
Donors can commit to small monthly payments over 5, 7, or 10 years:
- $1 per day for 5 years
- $2 per day for 7 years
- $3 per day for 10 years
