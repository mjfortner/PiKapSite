About Pi-Kap

Democracy. Equality. Loyalty. Service.

Kappa Pi Kappa Society, affectionately known as Pi-Kap, stands as the second oldest active fraternity at Dartmouth and the oldest active local fraternity in the United States.

Originally established as a literary and debate society dedicated to the principles of democracy, equality of opportunity, and loyalty to Dartmouth, the organization transitioned to a social fraternity in 1905 while maintaining its foundational commitments.

Today, our mission emphasizes brotherhood diversity across different backgrounds, charitable and social contributions to Dartmouth College, and long-term alumni engagement and dedication.

Our History

Over 180 years of brotherhood and tradition

Our Founders

On July 13, 1842, three visionaries separated from the United Fraternity to establish their own organization:

Harrison Hobart
Stephen Gordon Nash
John Dudley Philbrick
1842

Founded

Established on July 13 as Kappa Kappa Kappa (Tri-Kap) at Dartmouth College

1860

The Hall

Located at "The Hall" at the present Hopkins Center for the Arts site until 1894

1894

Parker House

Relocated to Parker House at the current Silsby Hall location

1905

Social Fraternity

Transitioned from literary and debate society to social organization

1923

Webster Avenue

Moved to our current home at 1 Webster Avenue, establishing the main Hall

2016

Modernization

Hall underwent expansion and modernization

2022

New Name

Changed from Kappa Kappa Kappa to Kappa Pi Kappa, affirming our values of equality and inclusion

Our members have included war heroes, business and political leaders, and contributors to communities globally across nearly two centuries.
